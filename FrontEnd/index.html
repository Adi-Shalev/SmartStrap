science Medical Science

Blast-Induced Hearing Loss (BIHL)

BIHL typically creates a sudden, irreversible "notch" in the audiogram, most commonly around 4000Hz. This missing frequency severely impacts speech intelligibility, making consonants like 's', 'f', and 'th' difficult to hear.

Sensory Substitution

The Smart Strap uses a principle called sensory substitution. By converting the exact missing 4kHz acoustic energy into tactile vibrations (haptics) on the skin, we bypass the damaged cochlear hair cells entirely.

Neuroplasticity & Brain Remapping

This training game leverages Hebbian learning ("neurons that fire together, wire together"). By simultaneously playing the muffled audio and triggering the haptic pulse, your auditory and somatosensory cortex begin to associate the vibration with the missing sound. Over time, your brain actually learns to "hear" the vibration, unconsciously filling in the blanks in human speech!
